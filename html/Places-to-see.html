Places To See

#1: Notre-Dame Cathedral

Due to the significant damage sustaned by a fire on April 15, 2019, The Cathedral remains closed until further notice.

#2: Musée du Louvre

Regarded as one of the best art museums in the world. Opened in 1793 and has over 35,000 pieces of art.

#3: Eiffel Tower

Constructed in 1889 for the World's Fair, the Eiffel Tower was always meant to be temporary but was kept around due to its wireless transmission uses.

#4: Arc de Triomphe

The towering Arc de Triomphe was commissioned by Napoléon to honor the Grande Armee during the Napoleonic Wars.

#5: Jardin des Tuileries

The Jardin des Tuileries is a free public garden that spans approximately 55 acres. It was initially intended for the use of the royal family and court, but has been open to the public since the 17th century.

#6: Le Marais

With all of its cobblestone streets, stately stone architecture and tucked away courtyards, it's easy to feel as if you're strolling through medieval Paris.

#7: Sacred Heart Basilica of Montmartre

As blanched as it may appear on the outside, the basilica's interior glitter with France's largest mosaic, depicting Jesus rising alongside the Virgin Mary and Joan of Arc.

#8: Musée d'Orsay

Housed in a former railway station along the Left Bank, the Musée d'Orsay is regarded for its rich collection of beautiful works. You'll see paintings by French artists like, Monet, Cezanne, Van Gogh, and many others.

#9: Luxembourg Gardens

The Luxembourg Gardens provide ample green space for sun-soaking and people-watching, plus there are plenty of activities to keep kids entertained.
